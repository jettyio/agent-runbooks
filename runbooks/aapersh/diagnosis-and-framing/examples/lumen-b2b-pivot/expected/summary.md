# Strategic Diagnosis — Summary

## Overview
- **Subject**: Lumen — consumer subscription mobile app (guided meditation & sleep); $6.5M ARR, 78,000 paying subscribers, 40 employees, 20 months runway
- **Decision framed**: Should Lumen redirect resources into a B2B corporate-wellness product to chase two inbound employer LOIs, or double down on fixing the consumer subscription funnel?
- **Date**: 2026-06-10

## The Read (answer-first)

Lumen's growth is structurally stalled because monthly churn of 8.5% consumes every subscriber acquired, compressing LTV/CAC to 1.45x and making the paid acquisition engine nearly value-neutral. The B2B opportunity is a real demand signal but is premature: building enterprise infrastructure (SSO, admin, reporting) would take 6–12 months and consume the same engineering capacity needed to fix the consumer leaks — and the LOIs are unvalidated on price and deal structure. Before committing to either path, Lumen needs two quick empirical tests (30 days each) to determine whether the LOIs are fundable and whether churn is fixable.

## Binding Constraint

Monthly subscriber churn at 8.5% — it makes the growth equation zero-sum, destroys LTV, and renders paid acquisition treadmill spending rather than compounding investment. All other problems (conversion decline, paid concentration, B2B infrastructure gap) are secondary.

## Recommendation

**Test first** — run a structured LOI price/pilot validation with both employers and a churn root-cause diagnosis in parallel (30 days, minimal cost) before allocating engineering to either path. What would change the verdict: if both LOI employers commit to a pilot at ≥$8/seat/month → shift to B2B-led redesign; if churn root cause is identified as content exhaustion or onboarding friction → proceed with consumer fix; if both tests fail → the business needs a fundamental rethink of its product strategy within the remaining runway.

## Top Open Question

What is actually driving monthly churn at 8.5%? Without exit-survey and engagement-cohort data to isolate the cause (content exhaustion vs. price sensitivity vs. competitive switching), every proposed fix — consumer or B2B — is a hypothesis rather than a solution.
