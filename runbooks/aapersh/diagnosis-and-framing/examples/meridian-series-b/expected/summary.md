# Strategic Diagnosis — Summary

## Overview
- **Subject**: Meridian Health Software — vertical SaaS for independent dental practices
- **Decision framed**: Should Meridian raise a $40M Series B to fund an upmarket move into DSOs, or is there a higher-leverage path given the current numbers?
- **Date**: 2026-06-10

## The Read (answer-first)

Meridian is in structural, not cyclical, deceleration: every dimension of its growth formula — acquisition win rate, CAC, NRR, and gross logo churn — is simultaneously deteriorating while a well-capitalized competitor offers a free substitute in the core segment. The proposed $40M upmarket bet into DSOs is premature: Meridian's own two DSO pilots churned within six months citing the same specific product gaps, and no evidence exists that those gaps have been addressed or scoped. The board is being asked to price a bet that has not yet been derisked enough to size.

## Binding Constraint

Competitive free substitution is eroding both win rates and retention in the core independent-practice segment, while the natural upmarket escape route (DSOs) requires product capabilities — multi-location reporting and SSO — that two real-world pilots have confirmed are absent and apparently un-scoped for remediation.

## Recommendation

**Test first** — conduct a 90-day evidence sprint (engineering scoping of DSO product gaps, structured market discovery with 15–20 DSO buyers, core ARR stress modeling) before committing capital at Series B scale; if those tests return affirmative results, the appropriate raise is $12–18M for a focused DSO MVP and limited enterprise pilot, not $40M for a full transformation whose premise is currently unvalidated. The verdict changes to Proceed if: (1) engineering confirms DSO gaps are closable in under 12 months for under $4M, (2) at least one-third of DSO prospects are actively seeking a new vendor, and (3) a DSO design partner signs an LOI.

## Top Open Question

What is the engineering scope — time and cost to production-ready — for multi-location reporting and SSO? This single answer determines whether the DSO upmarket move is executable within the capital envelope of any Series B and sets the minimum viable raise size.
